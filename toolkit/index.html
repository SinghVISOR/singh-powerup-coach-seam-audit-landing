  • You can give the toolkit four working sessions of real attention over two to four weeks.

You should not buy this if —

  • Your problem is inside a single function and you have not yet established that the chain is the unit of analysis. Start with the Field Kit and the SEAMline paper first.
  • You are looking for a quick template to drop into a slide deck. This is an instrument, not a graphic.
  • You already know the room is politically incapable of honest scoring. The Seam Audit is the right next move.
  • You expect the toolkit to do the diagnostic work for you. It will not. It is a structured instrument; the operator's judgment carries the work.

Most operators finish the cube walk in week one and spend weeks two through four on evidence, weighting, and the Diagnostic Summary.
