FAQ

What buyers ask before purchasing.

How is this different from the Fracture Diagnostic Toolkit?
The Fracture Diagnostic is a snapshot: where is the chain broken right now? Six phases × four parties × three fracture types = a 72-cell diagnostic. The Seam Maturity Model is the trajectory: how mature is the organization at NOT breaking the chain, and what does the climb look like? Five levels × four parties × thirty process areas. You can use either alone. Used together, they close a real diagnostic loop.
Is this related to BPMM or CMMI?
The Seam Maturity Model takes design inspiration from the CMMI lineage and the OMG Business Process Maturity Model, but it is purpose-built for multi-party value chains, not software organizations or single-org process management. The 4-parties layer is the unique addition — BPMM and CMMI treat the organization as monolithic; SMM treats it as a chain.
How long does it take to score?
Three weeks of intermittent effort to establish a defensible baseline. The scoring engine has 120 cells across 30 process areas × 4 parties. Each score requires evidence at 3 or above. Done honestly, that pace is what reality permits; done in a single 90-minute session, the score will be wrong.
Do I need both this and the Fracture Diagnostic Toolkit?
No. Either is genuinely sufficient on its own. The Worked Example included with this toolkit shows the chain running SMM standalone for nine months. If you can buy both, the diagnose-then-climb pairing is more powerful — but the toolkit is not designed as half of a forced bundle.
Can I share the toolkit with my consulting firm or my advisory client?
The single-organization license covers internal use by employees and engaged advisors of one legal entity. If you want to use SMM across multiple client engagements, the firm-wide license is the right purchase — email hello@theseamlinemethod.com. Verbatim quotation up to 250 words in client memos is permitted under any tier with attribution.
What if I score my chain and discover I'm at Level 1?
The toolkit is sufficient for the Level 1-to-2 climb. The Workbook Part VII.2 carries the four-play climb sequence. Most operators climbing from Level 1 stall before they start because they begin without an executive sponsor; the playbook addresses this explicitly. If your situation is more complicated than self-led work can handle, the Seam Audit is the supported next step.
